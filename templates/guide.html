Neural Training Protocols

Training operations build the core intuition of the neural network. Generating optimal weights requires feeding the agent historical epochs across calculated momentum windows.

Parallel Execution Engine

Because QuantTrader uses isolated `subprocess.Popen` protocols in the background, you can dispatch unrestricted and concurrent Reinforcement Learning jobs simultaneously without thread locking. Scaling is bounded strictly by your physical CPU limits.

Background A/B Tournaments

Every night precisely at 4:15 PM EST (post-market), QuantTrader will automatically run background A/B tournament evaluations to rank all training jobs against real-world test sets while the stock market is closed.

Training Timeline vs. Observation Window

Training Timeline (Days): The macro dataset size. This dictates how far back in time the engine will download historical market data for the AI to train against (e.g., 365 Days means it will learn by simulating trades through the entire past year of market volatility).

Observation Window (Days): The micro context size. This is how many consecutive past days the AI is allowed to "look at" at any single moment to make its immediate prediction (e.g., A window of 10 means the AI analyzes the previous 10 days of price action to decide whether to Buy or Sell today).

Feature Sets & Strategies

When training a model, you must select a Feature Set. This defines the mathematical indicators the neural network will analyze to make predictions. Here is a breakdown of the available intelligence protocols:

Alpha Discovery

Indicators: SMA (20, 50, 200), RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, ATR.

A balanced, omni-directional strategy. Looks for broad market inefficiencies and standard technical alignments.

When to use: Use this as your standard baseline on large-cap index funds like SPY or QQQ when the market is behaving normally with predictable swings.

Volatility Breakout

Indicators: Bollinger Bands, ATR, ADX, Momentum.

Aggressively targets explosive price movements and shatters in standard resistance levels.

When to use: Highly effective on extremely volatile assets (like Crypto: BTC, SOL) or during major news-driven market events and earnings seasons.

Intraday Reversal

Indicators: RSI, Stochastic, Williams %R, CCI.

Contrarian protocol that seeks overbought/oversold exhaustion points to bet against the immediate trend.

When to use: Deploy this during "choppy", flat, or non-trending markets where prices bounce back and forth between established support and resistance lines.

Macro Trend

Indicators: SMA (50, 200), EMA (21, 55), Ichimoku Cloud.

Patience-heavy trend-following system. Ignores short-term noise and only executes when long-term moving averages align.

When to use: Use with a large window size (20+) on fundamentally strong assets during long bull runs to capture massive multi-week swings.

Institutional Value

Indicators: VWAP, OBV, MFI, Volume.

Follows the money. Analyzes volume footprints to detect large institutional accumulation or distribution.

When to use: Excellent for trading heavy-volume blue-chip stocks (AAPL, MSFT) or index ETFs where institutional whales drive the price action.

Growth Momentum

Indicators: ROC, MACD Histogram, ADX, PSAR.

High-octane trend rider. Buys assets that are already moving fast and attempts to surf the momentum before it slows down.

When to use: Deploy on tech stocks or cryptocurrencies that are actively in "hype cycles" or experiencing rapid adoption, but be prepared for quick stop-losses.

Neural Hyperparameter Profiles

Beyond data features, the neural network requires a tuning profile (Hyperparameters) to define its behavior, learning rate, and risk appetite. These profiles dictate how aggressively the AI chases profits vs. preserves capital.

High Frequency Alpha

Params: LR: 0.003, Gamma: 0.90, Profit: +15, Loss: -20

Aggressive learning rate with a low gamma. The model prioritizes immediate short-term rewards (scalping) over long-term strategic positioning.

Kinetic Execution

Params: LR: 0.002, Gamma: 0.92, Profit: +12, Loss: -15

Fast execution designed for intraday momentum. Slightly more forward-looking than HFA, but still highly reactive to immediate volume spikes.

Long Horizon Growth

Params: LR: 0.0008, Gamma: 0.98, Profit: +8, Loss: -10

Very high gamma and low learning rate. The model learns slowly and heavily values future rewards, holding positions through short-term noise for massive macro gains.

Macro Rider

Params: LR: 0.001, Gamma: 0.96, Profit: +6, Loss: -8

A swing-trading profile. Catches multi-day or multi-week trends efficiently without over-trading.

Balanced Matrix

Params: LR: 0.001, Gamma: 0.95, Profit: +5, Loss: -8

The default, jack-of-all-trades profile. Stable learning rate, standard discount factor. Recommended for most Alpha Discovery runs.

Capital Preservation

Params: LR: 0.0005, Gamma: 0.98, Profit: +3, Loss: -5

Highly risk-averse. Extreme penalty ratio ensures the model will only execute a trade if the mathematical probability of loss is near zero.

  • Dynamic Hard-Loss Caps (12%): Instead of relying on manual <input> limits, the backend autonomously establishes a maximum 12% portfolio downside barrier based on initial equity upon boot. If the algorithmic cluster plummets past this 12% peak draw threshold, all sockets are permanently severed to protect the primary capital.
  • 1-Cent Defensive Mechanics: The execution wrapper algorithmically forces an absolute sell condition if the global Alpaca state crosses exactly $0.01 threshold per share to immediately secure trailing profits dynamically.
